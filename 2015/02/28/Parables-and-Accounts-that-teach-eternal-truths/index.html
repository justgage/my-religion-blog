Parables and Accounts that teach eternal truths

Sat Feb 28 2015

Understanding principles

Jesus’ parables are not always easy to understand, I’m going to go through 5 today and hopefully gain a better understanding of them!

The unjust steward (Luke 16:1-12)

This a bit of a weird one. It basically sounds like there’s this person “the unjust steward”
who mannages the money and property of a rich man. Somone tells the rich man that he’s
recklessly squandering his master’s property. So the rich man comes down on him then
the steward goes to some of his masters debtors and offered them huge discounts. For
some reason the master was very pleased with this.

From the commentary I’ve read it seems that the Lord was pointing some things about finances. Like
how we need to still make sure we give careful attention to our temporal affairs and we need to
be friends with people that are not members of the church beccause we never know when they will
help bring about good.

Lazarus and the rich man - Luke 16:19-31

Another rich man! In this one there’s lazarus who’s a poor begar that lives off crubs that fall from the rich man’s table.
The rich man is well, rich and thus has the many benifits of that. They both die. Lazarus goes to heaven and is confortable
and no longer crippled. The rich man is not so lucky and is torrmented to the point that even a drop of water from the from
Lazarus would be welcome. So we see their roles are reversed. Which goes back to the “last shall be first and the first shall
be last” thing.

I think one of the main things that we can learn from this is that don’t think that this life’s conforts are the most important thing!
Because they will soon end and the eternity will begin where we have to live with the person we’ve become in this life. No more cheating
the system, things will finally be fair.

The ten lepers - Luke 17:11-19

This is one of my favorites as a child. I think perhaps because of it’s ease in understanding. How it goes is Christ finds 10 leapers
and they ask to be healed, christ tells them to go see the preists and on their way they get healed! However only one came back
to Jesus and thanked him (the other’s where probably partying with their friends or somthing.) Jesus made an interesting comment about
the one,

19 And he said unto him, Arise, go they way: they faith hath made thee whole.

I think it’s interesting to note that while some may be healed this man was made whole. The verse seems to indicate that wholeness is
different than cleanlyness. Both are most cirtantly important but I think he does show that they change has gone more than skin deep
with the greatful man then with the others.

The parable of the unjust judge - Luke 18:1-8

Basically the story goes, there’s an unjust judge that has this widow who is pleading with him to advenge her. He resists at first then
decides that although he is not affraid of God or man but he’s affraid that she will continue to annoy him till he gives in so he does.

The pourpose is explicitly stated at the beginning of the verse:

1 And he spake a parable unto them to this end, that men ought always to pray, and not faint;

So as the widow was a great example of how persistant we should be. Even a bad judge will eventually listen to her how much more quickly
will God listen to us. It shows faith to not continue to belive that God will come through which I think Jesus was trying to show.

The parable of the Pharisee and the publican. - Luke 18:9-14

This one is about a Pharisee and a publican that both go to pray. THe Pharisee basically prays, “Thanks that I’m not a horrible sinner
like that other guy” meanwhile the publican says

And the publican, standing afar off, would not lift up so much his eyes unto heaven, but smote upon
his breast, saying, God be merciful to me a sinner.

I think this is very poignant. We need to not think that we’ve made it we need to realize that we desprately need God’s help and plead to
him for his help.
